Exercise 5.2.1

In HIRE-ASSISTANT, assuming that the candidates are presented in a random order, what is the probability that you hire exactly one time? What is the probability that you hire exactly $n$ times?

Given that there are $n!$ ways that $n$ candidates can be ordered and that the event, when we hire exactly once, $E$, happens when the best candidate is on the first position, then we have $(n - 1)!$ possibilities where the best candidate is placed first, then the probability of hiring exactly once is: \begin{align} Pr\{E\} &= \frac{(n-1)!}{n!} \\ Pr\{E\} &= \frac{1}{n} \end{align}

On the other hand, the event when we hire $n$ times $W$, happens only when the order of the candidates is in ascending order, then $$ Pr\{W\} = \frac{1}{n!} $$
