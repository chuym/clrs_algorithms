Exercise 5.2.2

In HIRE-ASSISTANT, assuming that the candidates are presented in random order, what is the probability that you hire exactly twice?

We know that the chance of hiring once is: $Pr\{A\} = \frac{1}{n}$ hiring twice means that we have to hire once in the subset $1 \leq i \lt n$, then for a particular $i$, the chance to hire once is $Pr\{B_i\} = \frac{1}{i}$, then the probability of both happening is: \begin{align} Pr\{A \cap B_i\} &= Pr\{A\} \cdot Pr\{B_i\} \\ Pr\{A \cap B_i\} &= \frac{1}{n} \cdot \frac{1}{i} \\ \end{align} That is the probability of hiring twice for a particular event $B_i$ we want the probability for all $B$, then we have: \begin{align} Pr\{B\} &= \sum_{i=1}^{n-1} Pr\{B_i\} \\ Pr\{B\} &= \sum_{i=1}^{n-1} \frac{1}{i} \\ \end{align} Then the probability of hiring twice, for any $B$ is: \begin{align} Pr\{A \cap B\} &= Pr\{A\} \cdot Pr\{B\} \\ Pr\{A \cap B\} &= \frac{1}{n} \sum_{i=1}^{n-1} \frac{1}{i} \end{align}
