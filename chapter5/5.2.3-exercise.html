Exercise 5.2.3

Use indicator random variables to compute the expected value of the sum of $n$ dice.

The possible outcomes of rolling a 6-sided die is given by the set $S = \{1, 2, 3, 4, 5, 6\}$, with a probability of picking any value in the set of $1/6$ Let $R$ be the value obtained from rolling a 6-sided die and $X$ be the random variable containing the value of the roll: $$ X_r = I\{R\} = \{1, 2, 3, 4, 5, 6 \} $$ The expected value obtaining after rolling the die is given by: \begin{split} E\left[X_r\right] &= E\left[I\{R\}\right] \\ &= \sum_{i=1}^{6} \frac{1}{6} \cdot i \\ &= \frac{1}{6} \cdot \sum_{i=1}^{6} i \\ &= \frac{1}{6} \cdot \frac{6(6+1)}{2} \\ &= \frac{1}{6} \cdot 21 \\ &= 3.5 \end{split} Then the sum of $n$ dice rolled is given by \begin{align} E[N] &= E\left[\sum_{i=1}^{n} X_r\right] \\ &= \sum_{i=1}^{n} E[X_r] \\ &= \sum_{i=1}^{n} 3.5 \\ &= n \cdot 3.5 \end{align}
