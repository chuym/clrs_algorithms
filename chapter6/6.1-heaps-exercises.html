Exercises 6.1

  1. What are the minimum and maximum numbers of elements in a heap of height $h$?

    The maximum number of elements of a heap of height $h$ is given by the function $M$ $$ M(h) = 2^{h-1} $$ The minimum number of elements of a heap of height $h$ is given by the function $m$ $$ m(h) = 2^{h-2} + 1 $$

  2. Show that an n-element heap has height $\lfloor \text{lg } n \rfloor$.

    We can show this by noting that for any element $i$ in the heap, that is not the root, we can find its parent with the function $P$: $$ P(i) = \lceil i/2 \rceil $$ Then, we can calculate the height with the recurrence $H$: $$ H(n) = \begin{cases} 1 &\mbox{if } n \equiv 1 \\ H(n/2) + 1 &\mbox{otherwise} \end{cases} $$ By the master theorem, we have $a = 1$, $b = 2$, $f(n) = 1$, case 2 applies as follows: $$ f(n) = \Theta(n^{\text{log}_ba}) \\ 1 = \Theta(n^{\text{log}_21}) \\ 1 = \Theta(n^0) \\ 1 = \Theta(1) \\ 1 = 1 $$ Then, the recurrence solution is $\Theta(\text{lg }n)$
